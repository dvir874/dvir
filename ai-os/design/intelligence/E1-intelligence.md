## Chief of Staff | 2026-06-26

## Executive Summary

The Marketing Experience is the product's acquisition engine. It converts strangers into customers. The design must answer one question before the visitor scrolls: "Is this beautiful enough to trust with my wedding?"

**Key insight:** The target audience (recently engaged Israeli couples, 25–35) is experiencing one of the most emotionally charged moments of their lives. They are not evaluating software — they are choosing a partner for their most important day. The marketing design must match that emotional weight.

---

## Company Standards Established — E1

### Standard 1 — Brand Narrative Before Features
The landing page hero communicates the brand story ("the most beautiful") before any feature is mentioned. Features are secondary evidence; beauty is the primary promise.

**Rule:** Marketing copy leads with emotional claim → product reality → features. Never: feature list → claim.

### Standard 2 — Real Wedding Photography as Primary Visual
Marketing photography must be real or photorealistic wedding venue/reception photography. Abstract gradients, geometric patterns, or generic imagery are rejected.

**Photography specification for E1 hero:**
- Scene: warm candlelit wedding reception
- Time of day: golden hour or evening
- Warmth: amber/gold color temperature
- Subject: decorated tables, guests, flowers, atmosphere (not portraits of specific people)
- Technical: horizontal aspect ratio (for hero cropping)

### Standard 3 — Phone Mockup with Real Product Screen
The product preview section must use a realistic phone mockup showing the actual RSVP or dashboard screen — not a generic screenshot or illustration.

**This is the most powerful marketing asset.** Potential customers see exactly what their guests will experience. No description is needed.

### Standard 4 — Social Proof Anchored to Specific Numbers
Social proof claims use specific, credible numbers: "800+ זוגות" not "hundreds of couples." The number should be updated with real data at launch and maintained quarterly.

## Anti-Patterns Documented — E1

| Anti-Pattern | Why Rejected | Correct Approach |
|---|---|---|
| Abstract/gradient hero | Doesn't show the wedding world | Real wedding photography |
| Feature-first copy | Functional, not emotional | "הכי יפה" before features |
| Generic testimonials | Less credible than none | Real customer quotes with names + dates |
| English CTA in Hebrew context | Breaks authenticity | Hebrew everywhere |
| Different visual language from product | Jarring transition on signup | Exact same brand tokens |
| Vague social proof ("hundreds") | Less credible | Specific numbers ("800+") |

## Funnel Intelligence — E1

### The Journey Map (marketing → signup)

```
Instagram/Google ad (warm photography)
    ↓
Landing page hero (same warm photography, brand confirmation)
    ↓
Product preview section (I can see exactly what my guests will see)
    ↓
Features grid (yes, it does everything I need)
    ↓
CTA — התחילו בחינם
    ↓
Registration (onboarding wizard)
    ↓
First dashboard view
    ↓
"This is what I imagined."
```

Every step in this funnel must maintain the same emotional tone. If any step feels colder or more generic than the previous, the funnel breaks.

### Highest Risk Drop-Off Point
Between landing page and registration: the registration page is currently undesigned. If registration feels like a generic form after the beautiful landing page, conversion will suffer significantly.

**Priority for next design wave: Registration page.**

## Business Value Assessment — E1

The marketing redesign has direct revenue impact:
- Higher conversion rate from visitor → trial signup
- Better brand positioning vs. Israeli competitors (Zola-style platforms)
- Cohesive brand story from first touch to wedding day

**Estimated impact:** A 10% improvement in landing page conversion (from, say, 3% to 3.3%) compounds across all marketing channels. At 10,000 monthly visitors, that is 30 additional trials per month.
