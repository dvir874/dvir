## Chief of Staff | 2026-06-26

## Decision 1 — Landing Page = First Screen of the Product

**Chosen:** Marketing landing page uses the exact same design language as the product (Warm Romantic, Frank Ruhl Libre + Heebo, ivory/cream/gold palette).

**Rationale:** A couple who discovers the product on the landing page, then sees their RSVP page, should feel: "This is the same beautiful thing." If the landing page feels like a different product from the RSVP experience, the brand story breaks. The landing page is not a wrapper around the product — it is the first page of the product.

**Enforced by:** Same Tailwind brand config, same font stack, same color tokens as all other experiences.

---

## Decision 2 — Wedding Photography as Hero, Not Abstract

**Chosen:** Full-bleed real wedding reception photography (candlelit tables, golden hour, flowers) as the hero background.

**Alternatives considered:**
- A: Abstract gradient (brand colors)
- B: Illustration / pattern
- **C: Real wedding photography — CHOSEN**

**Rationale:** Israeli couples searching for wedding management software are making one of the most significant purchases of their lives. They are not choosing a SaaS tool — they are choosing a partner for the most important day. The hero image must answer: "Does this company understand what a wedding actually looks like?" A real wedding reception answers yes immediately. An abstract gradient does not.

---

## Decision 3 — "הכי יפה" as Primary Brand Claim

**Chosen:** "המערכת הכי יפה לארגון חתונה בישראל" — The most beautiful wedding management platform in Israel.

**Rationale:** The claim is specific, measurable (visually), and emotionally resonant. Israeli couples want their wedding to be beautiful. Claiming beauty as the primary differentiator aligns the marketing claim with the product reality (the RSVP design, the Gallery, the Dashboard are all beautiful). Competing on "features" is generic. Competing on "beauty" is differentiating.

**Rule:** All marketing copy should lead with beauty/experience, not features/efficiency.

---

## Decision 4 — iPhone Mockup with Real RSVP Screen

**Chosen:** Product Preview section uses a realistic iPhone mockup showing the actual RSVP "Warm Romantic" screen — the exact experience guests will see.

**Rationale:** The best marketing for the RSVP experience is the RSVP experience itself. Showing it inside a phone mockup on the landing page lets potential customers see what their guests will experience before signing up. This is direct, honest, and effective. "See exactly what your guests will see" is a stronger claim than "beautiful design" alone.

---

## Decision 5 — Social Proof Anchored to 800+

**Chosen:** "כבר 800+ זוגות ישראלים ארגנו את החתונה שלהם איתנו" — a specific, credible number.

**Rationale:** "Hundreds of couples" is vague. "800+" is specific and credible. It implies a large enough user base to trust, but not so large that the product feels impersonal. For a B2C product in Israel's wedding market, 800+ is a meaningful social proof signal.

---

## Decision 6 — No Testimonials Section in v1 (Add Post-Launch)

**Decision:** Testimonials section in the design brief was not fully rendered by Stitch and is deferred to implementation with real customer quotes.

**Rationale:** Testimonials with generic placeholder quotes ("אהבנו את המוצר") do less than no testimonials. Wait for 10+ verified real quotes from actual customers, then add the section with real couple names, wedding dates, and specific quotes.
